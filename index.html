About CSI DYPIEMR Student Chapter

The Computer Society of India (CSI) Student Branch of Dr. D.Y. Patil Institute of Engineering, Management and Research (DYPIEMR), established in 2014 is an active student organization which organizes a number of technical activities including workshops, competitions, guest lectures etc. for its student members. Under the guidance of Department of Computer Engineering of DYPIEMR, and is run by a Student Council, and faculty from the department. The CSI DYPIEMR Student Chapter gives students the opportunity to grow in the field of IT.

About CSI

Established in 1965, the Computer Society of India, or CSI is the first and largest body of computer professionals in India. It is a non-profit professional meet to exchange views and information learn and share ideas regarding advancement of theory and practice of Computer Engineering and Technology Systems, Science and Engineering, Information Processing and many more. The national body now has 72 chapters across India, 512 student branches, and over 110000 members, including noted IT industry leaders, scientists, and dedicated mathematicians.

  • Regularly organizes workshops, seminars conventions, and technical talks for the benefit of professionals and users of IT apart from conducting development programs for professionals, researchers and students.
  • Works closely with other industry associations, government bodies and academia to ensure that the benefits of IT advancement ultimately percolate down to every single citizen of India.
  • Extends funding support for research projects as well as funds visits of researchers presenting papers at international conferences.

Objectives


  • To inspire and nurture students, and help them to chalk out a career path.
  • To encourage students to participate in various types of technical events like workshops, symposiums etc.
  • To bring about the technical development of students by organizing seminars, workshops and other activities.
  • To improve non-technical abilities of students by engendering good communication skills, managerial abilities, presentation skills and team work.
